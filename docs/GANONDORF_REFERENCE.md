# Ganondorf Reference

Use Ganondorf in _Super Smash Bros. Ultimate_ as the dedicated reference for realistic animation cheating that increases power without reading cartoony.

This is not a timing reference.
It is a body-steering and pose-cheating reference.

## Why Ganondorf

Ganondorf is a heavyweight whose attacks read as massive and authored.
The body cheats aggressively, but the model rarely looks rubbery.

What makes him useful here:

- hips and torso clearly lead power
- shoulders and chest compress before release
- silhouettes get larger through rotation and line of action
- contact reads are arrogant and readable
- the head usually stays disciplined instead of spearheading the attack

## Observation Targets

### Ballistic attacks

Use these to study realistic cheating on fast, snappy moves:

- `Forward aerial`
- `Wizard's Foot`

What to steal:

- snap is preserved
- body mass still supports the limb
- directional extension is subtle
- recoil remains part of move identity

What to avoid:

- turning the strike into a display pose
- long contact holds
- visible rubber scaling

### Heavy attacks

Use these to study realistic cheating on committed attacks:

- `Forward smash`
- `Up tilt`
- `Down smash`

What to steal:

- visible compression before release
- hips-to-torso-to-limb force hierarchy
- large silhouette change at apex or contact
- readable, screenshot-worthy contact frames

What to avoid:

- floating limb-only attacks
- face-first commitment
- obvious cartoon stretch

## Rules For Pose Lab

Treat Ganondorf as the benchmark for `realistic body cheat`.

Do copy:

- torso compression before release
- hips-first power generation
- shoulder and torso exaggeration over noodle-limb exaggeration
- restrained head motion
- bigger silhouette at the decisive read

Do not copy:

- exact frame timing
- exact move arcs
- game-specific hitstop assumptions

## Mapping To Current Grammar

- `FrontKick`: use Ganondorf as a ballistic reference. Preserve snap first, then add small torso/support-leg cheating.
- `AxeKick`, `AxleKick`, `LowBackKick`, `SpinningHighKick`: use Ganondorf as a heavy-attack reference. Emphasize compression, commitment, and silhouette.

## Pass/Fail Question

When a clip uses Smash-inspired steering, the viewer should think:

`That looked powerful.`

They should not think:

`That bone scaled.`
