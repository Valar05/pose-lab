# Smash-Inspired Realistic Animation Steering Guide

## Purpose

Apply the animation principles of Super Smash Bros. to realistic characters.

The goal is not cartoon animation and not realistic motion-capture fidelity. The goal is increased impact, readability, power, character, and visual clarity.

The audience should feel the animation. The audience should not notice the trick.

## Core Philosophy

Realistic characters are allowed to cheat. Exaggerate force, timing, and intention, not anatomy.

Good rule: `Feel bigger than reality.`

Bad rule: `Look like rubber.`

## Prime Directive

Never sacrifice move identity. Smash techniques should amplify the move, never replace it.

## Compression Before Power

Before powerful attacks, allow subtle body compression in hips, knees, torso, and shoulders.

Compression range: roughly 5-15 percent.

The body should look loaded, not squashed.

## Directional Extension

During explosive attacks, allow slight extension along the force vector.

Examples:
- Punch: shoulder extends, arm line lengthens
- Kick: hip-to-foot line lengthens
- Sword: weapon arc extends

Maximum stretch should usually stay around 5-10 percent and only last 1-2 frames.

Never create visible noodle limbs.

## Impact Compression

Preferred Smash technique. At contact, allow a brief compression of chest, shoulders, neck, and torso so the body appears to absorb its own force.

Prefer this over large attack stretching.

## Contact Freeze

Strong attacks should freeze for roughly 1-3 frames. The contact frame should be readable, powerful, and screenshot-worthy.

## Overshoot

After contact, allow slight continuation beyond the contact frame: `Contact -> Overshoot -> Recovery`.

This increases force perception and avoids robotic stopping.

## Recovery

Recovery is a combat action, not interpolation. The character should regain balance, restore guard, and re-center mass.

## Weight Hierarchy

Motion should flow: `Hips -> Torso -> Shoulders -> Limbs`.

The body creates power. The limb delivers power.

## Head Rules

The head should rarely lead motion. Prefer `Hips -> Torso -> Head`. The audience should follow the fist, foot, or weapon, not the face.

## Weapon Rules

Weapons may exaggerate more than bodies. Prefer weapon exaggeration over body exaggeration when realism matters.

## Ballistic Attack Rules

Applies to front kicks, jabs, thrusts, pokes, and quick cuts. Prioritize acceleration, snap, and recoil. Minimize anticipation holds and long contact holds. The move identity lives in velocity.

## Heavy Attack Rules

Applies to axe kicks, haymakers, shield bashes, heavy sword cuts, and smash-style heavies. Prioritize anticipation, compression, contact holds, and follow-through. The move identity lives in commitment.

## Silhouette Rule

Pause at anticipation, contact, and recovery. If the move cannot be identified instantly, improve the pose before improving the interpolation.

## Appeal Rule

Strong poses beat smooth motion. If forced to choose, choose readable pose over smooth transition.

## Agent Evaluation Checklist

Score 0-10:
- Move Identity
- Readability
- Weight
- Compression
- Impact
- Contact Pose
- Recovery
- Silhouette
- Smash Feel

Penalty conditions:
- Visible rubber limbs
- Cartoon deformation
- Lost move identity
- Head leading power generation

## Final Rule

The player should think: `That looked powerful.`

The player should never think: `That was stretched.`
